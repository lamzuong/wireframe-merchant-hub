01 · Why this matters

Restaurants today buy 5 disconnected tools and stitch them together.

A merchant's POS doesn't know their reservation system, the delivery app doesn't know the menu, the AI chatbot doesn't know the floor. Every tool re-asks the same data. Every staff member re-learns a different UI.

✅ What Merchant Hub does differently

  • Six services on one shared core (menu, customers, staff, promotion codes).
  • Pay only for what you use — buy more later, no migration.
  • Every AI surface speaks the same data: menu changes once, propagates everywhere.
  • Staff learn one UI instead of five.

🎯 The opportunity

  • Land merchants with a low-friction Starter — Website + Core data.
  • Grow account value as we add Table Mgmt → Contactless → Delivery → AI.
  • Two services are hybrid-priced (AI Concierge, Table Mgmt) — revenue scales with the merchant's success.
🎤 Talking points
  • This is a platform play, not a tool play — bundling becomes our moat.
  • The shared core is the technical wedge: every service reads the same menu, the same CDP, the same booking engine.
  • Two pricing shapes (flat + hybrid) means we capture upside without scaring the small merchant.

03 · How merchants buy

One tier + zero-or-more add-ons. Two pricing shapes.

Tiers are dynamic — defined at runtime in billing config, not hardcoded. Business can add a new tier, rename one, or move a service between bundle and add-on without code changes.

Shape 1

Subscription (flat fee)

Constant month-over-month. Value scales with usage but the price doesn't.

🌐 Website Builder 📊 AI Management 📱 Contactless 🛵 Delivery
Shape 2

Hybrid (subscription + PAYG overage)

Subscription unlocks the service + a monthly quota. Going over the quota bills as PAYG at a defined rate.

💬 AI Concierge · per AI conversation 🪑 Table Mgmt · per reservation

Adding a service later — frictionless

  1. Merchant clicks a locked entry in the sidebar OR opens Browse services.
  2. Sees the service value prop, price, what's included.
  3. Confirms purchase → billing webhook updates entitlements.
  4. Sidebar and dashboard reflect the new service immediately. No re-login.
  5. First-run wizard for that service runs on first open.

Non-payment lifecycle (the only path that removes a service)

payment fails →
PAST DUEBanner · still usable

LOCKEDUI hidden · data kept

A successful payment at any point returns the account to Active immediately. Locked-state public surfaces degrade to read-only — menu visible, ordering/booking disabled.

04 · Hub UX model

Persistent sidebar + per-service top tabs. One pattern across every service.

Staff switch services constantly through a shift. The sidebar never disappears — switching is always one click. Inside a service, the IA lives in top tabs (max ~6, with Settings always last).

📐 Layout principles

  • Top bar — location switcher, search, notifications, avatar. Same on every screen.
  • Hub sidebar — Dashboard, Services (active + locked), Shared data (Menu/Customers/Staff/Promo), Browse, Settings.
  • Service area — per-service top tabs, content swap below.
  • Last tab = Settings always, in every service.
  • Locked services stay in the sidebar dimmed with 🔒 — click routes to value-prop, not 404.

🏠 Default landing — unified dashboard

  • Per-service widgets pull KPIs + quick actions from each active service.
  • An "You don't have…" card recommends a missing service based on signals (e.g. "Add Delivery — diners search your menu but can't order in").
  • Widgets follow a fixed contract — hub doesn't know what each one renders.
  • Empty hub → guided onboarding (choose tier → add-ons → first-run wizards).

Service 1 of 6

Restaurant Website Builder

Drag-and-drop website builder tuned for the restaurant use case.

Who it's for

Restaurants without an existing website, or with a generic template that doesn't convert.

Starter and Operator tiers especially.

Pricing model

Subscription — included with every Hub tier.

No PAYG component; the value is constant month-over-month.

What it does

Lets the merchant build and maintain their own restaurant website without a developer. Pre-fills menu, hours, photos, and reviews from existing online presence (Foody / Google) so the merchant starts at 80% rather than zero. Hosts on Savyu infrastructure with SSL, mobile-responsive design, and basic SEO.

Key features

  • Templates designed for the restaurant use case (menu, gallery, location, reservations).
  • Auto-fills from existing presence (Foody, Google Business Profile).
  • Embedded reservation and order CTAs that route to Savyu products.
  • SEO basics: structured data, sitemap, mobile responsive.
  • Content updates flow from the Merchant CMS — update once, propagates here.
🖥 Demo: Website Builder · Site tab (pre-fill callout + live preview)
🎤 Talking points
  • The "80% to start" is the wedge — pre-filling from Foody and Google removes the cold-start problem that kills every other website builder.
  • Bundled with every tier means it's the always-present surface — drives stickiness even when other services lapse.
  • Demo flow: open Site → show pre-fill callout → toggle device frame (desktop/tablet/mobile) → click Publish → show pipeline modal.

Service 2 of 6

AI Concierge

Customer-facing AI assistant that takes bookings, answers questions, and handles inquiries.

Who it's for

Restaurants with high inquiry volume — popular spots, hotel restaurants, places with extensive menus or dietary complexity.

Operator and Grower tiers.

Pricing model

Hybrid: subscription for the Concierge product + PAYG for AI inference beyond included monthly quota.

Included quota scales with Hub tier.

What it does

Sits on the merchant's website, Zalo OA, WhatsApp, or other channels. Answers questions about menu, dietary options, hours, location, parking, kid-friendliness. Books reservations directly into the Booking Engine. Knows the menu cold (powered by the Content Engine). Hands off to humans for complex requests.

Key features

  • Multi-channel: web widget, Zalo, WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger.
  • Books reservations end-to-end (writes to the Booking Engine).
  • Knows menu, allergens, hours, policies (reads from the Content Engine).
  • Conversation transcripts feed the Unified CDP for end-customer profiles.
  • Escalation rules per merchant (e.g. "private events → always to a human").

📈 Insights

Service 3 of 6

AI Management System

Operations dashboard with intelligence layered on top — not just metrics, but recommendations.

Who it's for

Owner-operators and managers who run the day-to-day. Operator tier and above.

The "how is my business doing" daily check-in.

Pricing model

Subscription — included with Operator tier and above.

No PAYG; value scales with how often the merchant logs in, not with usage volume.

What it does

The default dashboard view inside the Hub. Shows yesterday's performance, today's outlook, and recommended actions. Uses Aggregated Data Intelligence to compare merchant performance against peers in their cuisine type and area. Surfaces "the next best action this week" rather than dumping raw metrics.

Key features

  • KPIs: covers, average ticket, online vs walk-in mix, repeat customer rate.
  • Peer benchmarks: "how do I compare to similar restaurants nearby?"
  • Daily recommendations: surfaced by the rule engine.
  • Anomaly detection: flag drops or spikes the merchant should know about.
  • Mobile-first — designed for the owner checking on their phone.
🖥 Demo: AI Management · Today (with mobile preview)
🎤 Talking points
  • The product is positioned as the operator's daily ritual — that's why it's mobile-first.
  • "Aggregated Data Intelligence" is our peer-benchmarking moat — gets stronger with every merchant we add.
  • Demo flow: Today tab → highlight the "Next best action this week" card (₫1.8M estimated impact) → click "Why this?" → show peer signals + confidence.
  • Connect to other services: anomaly says repeat-rate down → linked recommendation is to tighten Table Mgmt slots → one click jumps to Table Mgmt.

Service 4 of 6

Table Management System

Floor plan, reservation management, and table turnover tracking.

Who it's for

Restaurants with reservations and managed seating — sit-down restaurants, fine dining, popular casual spots.

Grower tier and above.

Pricing model

Hybrid: subscription for the product + PAYG fee per reservation processed beyond included monthly quota.

Included quota scales with Hub tier.

What it does

Visual floor plan that staff use during service to seat parties, track turnover, and coordinate with the kitchen. Reservations from any source (AI Concierge, website, Booking app, Giaodoan, walk-in) flow into one view. Staff see the night's plan and current state in one screen.

Key features

  • Drag-and-drop floor plan editor; multiple floor plans per merchant (lunch vs dinner, indoor vs patio).
  • Real-time table status: occupied, paying, turning, available.
  • Reservation queue from all sources unified (writes to and reads from the Booking Engine).
  • Turnover analytics: average seating duration by table size, day, time.
  • Mobile-friendly for hosts working off a tablet at the door.
🖥 Demo: Table Mgmt · Floor plan (live status visualization)
🎤 Talking points
  • Owns the Booking Engine — that's the architectural keystone. AI Concierge writes to it, Website widget writes to it, Public booking page writes to it. One source of truth for what's bookable.
  • Demo: Floor plan tab → show real-time table status (occupied/paying/turning/available) with live timers → side panel waitlist with one-click seating.
  • Sources tab shows the unified queue from 6 sources including Giaodoan (Vietnamese marketplace) + Savyu's own Booking app.

Service 5 of 6

Merchant CMS — Contactless

QR ordering at the table — kitchen routing, runner workflow, bill splitting.

Who it's for

Restaurants accepting dine-in orders via QR at the table — high-throughput casual, brunch, coffee, fast-casual.

Pricing model

Subscription. Add-on, or included in higher Hub tiers — defined dynamically in billing config.

What it does

Diners scan a QR at their table and order without flagging a waiter. Orders fire straight to the kitchen tagged with the table; runners deliver; the bill closes against the table session. Reduces wait, raises ticket size on add-ons.

Key features

  • Live orders kanban by table session — "Bill requested", "Runner needed", "Called waiter" surfaced inline.
  • QR generation and printing per table; table grouping into sections.
  • Configurable diner actions: call waiter, request bill, modify items, split bill, tip.
  • Kitchen routing rules per station (hot kitchen, cold, bar, coffee).
  • Service charge, tipping policy, table-session timeout.
🖥 Demo: Contactless · Live orders (kanban by table)
🎤 Talking points
  • Where it shines: high-throughput casual, brunch, coffee — places where waiting for service is the bottleneck.
  • The kanban-by-table framing is the difference from Delivery's kanban-by-order — Contactless thinks in sessions, not transactions.
  • Demo: Live orders → show "Bill requested" tag on a table → switch to Tables & QR tab → show QR cards with section colors.

Service 6 of 6

Merchant CMS — Delivery

Off-premise orders, drivers, zones, ETAs, dispatch.

Who it's for

Restaurants running off-premise orders — own delivery, pickup, or 3rd-party logistics fallback.

Pricing model

Subscription. Add-on, or included in higher Hub tiers — defined dynamically in billing config.

What it does

Manages every off-premise order from intake through delivery: live order kanban, driver dispatch, delivery zones, ETAs. Wired into the shared menu, customer, and promotion-code services — a delivery order is just another channel.

Key features

  • Live order kanban: new → preparing → ready → out for delivery → delivered.
  • Driver roster, live status and location on a map, assignment rules.
  • Delivery zones (polygon), per-zone fees, minimum order, ETA rules.
  • Auto-dispatch with manual override and 3rd-party logistics fallback (Grab Express, Ahamove, Lalamove).
  • Order channels including direct, GrabFood, ShopeeFood, Beamin — with per-channel commission tracking.
🖥 Demo: Delivery · Live orders + Drivers map
🎤 Talking points
  • Delivery is the highest-volume add-on potential — and the most ops-heavy. The kanban + driver map answer "what's happening right now" in one screen.
  • 3rd-party fallback is the unlock for merchants who don't want to hire drivers — Grab Express / Ahamove fill the gap.
  • Demo: Live orders kanban → ETA breach flag → Drivers tab → map with color-coded pins → Dispatch tab → auto-assign rules.
05 · Shared data + cross-cutting

The wedge isn't a service — it's the shared core.

Menu, customers, staff, and promotion codes are platform services, not features of one app. Every service reads from and writes to the same records. Change the menu once → AI Concierge knows new prices · Website rebuilds · Contactless QR landing updates · Delivery shows new items.

🍽️
Menu (Content Engine)
Items · categories · modifiers · per-channel pricing · allergens · availability schedules.
Read by all 6 services
👥
Customers (Unified CDP)
One profile per diner — orders, bookings, conversations, allergens, tags, spend.
Written to by Delivery, Contactless, Tables, Concierge
🧑‍🍳
Staff & Roles
Per-service RBAC — "Cashier" might get Contactless orders only. Audit log of every write.
Permissions enforced at the API, not just UI
🏷️
Promotion Code
Codes scoped to specific services (Delivery-only, Contactless-only, or any). Redemptions tracked.
Records redemptions per service
📅
Booking Engine
Owned by Table Mgmt. Driven by AI Concierge, Website widget, Public booking page, manual host stand.
Cross-UI writes · single source of truth
📡
Event bus
order.completed · booking.created · conversation.escalated. AI Management subscribes broadly for telemetry.
Loose coupling between services

Why this matters technically

  • One menu, six surfaces — Website, AI Concierge, Contactless QR, Delivery, Table Mgmt floor plan, and the public booking widget all consume the same Content Engine.
  • Customer recognition — when an AI Concierge chat is identified as a returning customer, the AI knows their allergies, past bookings, and average spend from the CDP automatically.
  • Loose coupling — services don't call each other directly. They emit events; subscribers react. Adding a 7th service later is additive, not a re-wire.
  • Audit by default — every write produces a Staff → Activity log entry with actor, action, before/after.
🖥 Demo: see the shared core in action → Concierge Knowledge tab
🎤 Talking points
  • The Concierge Knowledge tab is the best place to visualize the shared core — show how 8 sources auto-sync from platform services (menu, allergens, hours, CDP, bookings) vs 12 manual sources.
  • This is the moat — competitors can copy any single service, but rebuilding the shared-core architecture takes years.
  • It's also our pricing leverage — the more services a merchant has, the more value the shared core delivers, the more locked-in the relationship becomes.
